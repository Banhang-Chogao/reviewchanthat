Ho Chi Minh City eats like nowhere else in Southeast Asia. On one street you can queue at a plastic stool for a bowl of phở that has been perfected over decades; a block away, a chef is plating modern Vietnamese cuisine worthy of a Michelin star. When the **MICHELIN Guide arrived in Vietnam in 2023** — covering Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi — it put a global spotlight on both ends of that spectrum, and gave visitors a reliable map to the city's best tables. This guide walks you through the Michelin-recognised places worth planning a meal around, from the fine-dining highlight to the value-packed street-food gems, and how to fit them into your trip.

## How the MICHELIN Guide works in Saigon

Before you pick where to eat, it helps to know what the labels mean, because in Ho Chi Minh City they cover wildly different price points:

- **Michelin Star (★):** exceptional cooking; the destination-dining tier. Book ahead.
- **Bib Gourmand:** great food at a moderate price — in Saigon this is where the legendary street-food and local eateries live.
- **The MICHELIN Selected (the Plate):** inspector-recommended restaurants that don't (yet) carry a star or Bib but are still worth your time.

One important caveat up front: **the selection is refreshed every year.** Stars are added and occasionally lost, Bib Gourmand lists change, and restaurants move or close. Treat the names below as a starting point and confirm the current status on the [official MICHELIN Guide page for Ho Chi Minh City](https://guide.michelin.com/en/vn/ho-chi-minh-city/restaurants) before you build your evening around it.

## The starred table: Anan Saigon

The headline act is **Anan Saigon**, the restaurant that earned Ho Chi Minh City its Michelin star. Chef Peter Cuong Franklin reinterprets Vietnamese classics through a fine-dining lens — think elevated versions of familiar street dishes, served with serious technique in the heart of the old Chợ Cũ market area. It is the place to book if you want the full "Michelin in Saigon" experience: a tasting menu, a considered wine and cocktail list, and dishes that are unmistakably Vietnamese but reimagined.

Because it's the city's marquee starred restaurant, **reserve well ahead** — several days to a couple of weeks for weekends. Come hungry and curious rather than expecting a traditional white-tablecloth room; the appeal is the creativity, not formality.

## Bib Gourmand: where Saigon's soul lives

If you only chase stars, you'll miss the best part. Ho Chi Minh City's **Bib Gourmand** list is a love letter to Vietnamese street food — the bowls and plates locals actually eat, recognised for being both delicious and inexpensive. This is where a Michelin recommendation can cost you just a few dollars.

Expect the categories that define the city:

- **Phở and noodle soups** — the slow-simmered beef or chicken broths that people cross town for.
- **Cơm tấm (broken rice)** — grilled pork over rice, a Saigon institution, especially for breakfast and lunch.
- **Bún, hủ tiếu and regional specialities** — southern noodle dishes that reward the adventurous.
- **Bánh mì** — the crackly-crusted Vietnamese sandwich that needs no introduction.

Because the exact Bib Gourmand line-up shifts year to year, the smart move is to open the official guide, filter Ho Chi Minh City by **Bib Gourmand**, and pick two or three that are near your hotel or itinerary. Most of these spots don't take reservations, so go a little before the rush, bring cash, and be ready to share a table or a stool.

## How to plan a Michelin food trip in Saigon

A few practical tips to get the most out of it:

**Cluster by district.** Many of the best-known spots sit in or near **District 1**, which is also the most convenient base for visitors — another reason it tops our [reputable-hotel guide](/posts/how-to-choose-reputable-hotels-ho-chi-minh-city/). Staying central means you can walk or take a short ride to most of your meals.

**Book the splurge, wing the street food.** Lock in the starred or fine-dining reservation as the anchor of your trip, then leave the Bib Gourmand spots flexible — they're best enjoyed spontaneously, when you're hungry and nearby.

**Time your flights and stay smartly.** Peak food-tourism seasons overlap with the busiest travel windows, so sorting transport and accommodation early saves money and stress. Our companion guide on [booking cheap flights to Ho Chi Minh City on Trip.com](/posts/how-to-book-cheap-flights-to-ho-chi-minh-city-trip-com/) covers the timing tricks — and the same platform makes it easy to line up a well-located hotel near the restaurants you want to try.

**Pace yourself.** Portions are generous and the temptation is constant. Plan one "big" meal a day and graze on street food around it, rather than trying to do everything at once.
