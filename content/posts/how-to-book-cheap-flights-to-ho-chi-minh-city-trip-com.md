Airfare is usually the single biggest line in a Vietnam trip, and the gap between a well-timed booking and a last-minute panic buy can be enormous. The good news is that the same platform most travellers use to book their room — Trip.com — is also one of the easiest places to find a cheap flight, and there is a simple booking order that makes your flight *and* your hotel cheaper at the same time. ## First, know your airport and your season

Almost every visitor flies into **Tan Son Nhat International Airport (SGN)**, about 7 km from District 1. When you search, use **SGN** as your destination code so you don't accidentally price a connection through a distant hub. From there, price is mostly a function of *when* you fly.

Ho Chi Minh City has clear price seasons:

- **Cheapest (low/shoulder season):** roughly late February to April, and September to early November. Weather is warm, crowds are thinner, and fares soften.
- **Most expensive (peak):** the Tet (Lunar New Year) window, the April 30–May 1 national holiday, and the December year-end rush. Book these months as early as you realistically can.
- **Day of week:** mid-week departures (Tuesday–Thursday) are usually cheaper than Friday–Sunday. Shifting your trip by a day or two often saves more than any coupon.

A practical rule of thumb: for long-haul routes, start watching prices **two to three months out**, and don't wait for a "perfect" drop that may never come. For regional routes within Asia, three to six weeks ahead is often the sweet spot.

## The booking order that also cuts your hotel bill

Here is the part most travellers miss. On Trip.com, the flight and the hotel are not separate silos — they feed each other:

1. **Create your Trip.com account first** (before you shop), so any welcome offers, new-user coupons and Trip Coins are already attached when you check out.
2. **Book the flight to SGN first.** Completing a flight booking typically drops **hotel discount vouchers** and Trip Coins into your account wallet.
3. **Then book your hotel** — and *before you pay*, open the **Coupons / Wallet** section and apply any hotel voucher you just earned.

Done in that order, the flight booking effectively subsidises the room. You are not chasing a mythical "cheapest flight" in isolation; you are lowering the **total cost of the trip** — flight plus stay — which is the number that actually matters. Pair this with the hotel-selection advice in our [reputable-hotel guide](/posts/how-to-choose-reputable-hotels-ho-chi-minh-city/) and you get a good room *and* a lower bill.

## Where the real discounts come from

Beyond the flight-to-hotel voucher trick, three levers move the price the most:

**Watch Trip.com promotions.** Trip.com runs frequent flash sales, seasonal campaigns and app-only deals. The mobile app often surfaces prices and coupons you won't see on desktop, and app-exclusive discounts are common. If you have a flexible date range, the promo calendar can beat any manual haggling.

**Use flexible-date search.** Instead of locking one departure day, search a whole month or a ±3-day window. Seeing the price grid side by side almost always reveals a cheaper neighbouring date — that single click is the highest-value move in the whole process.

**Stack what you've earned.** Trip Coins accumulate with each booking and can offset future flights or hotels. New-user coupons, seasonal codes and the hotel vouchers from step 2 above can often be combined. Check the terms, but the habit of *always opening your wallet before paying* pays for itself quickly.
